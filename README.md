### Abstract

**An Interpretable Stacking Ensemble and Fuzzy Cognitive Map-Based Approach for Predicting Polycystic Ovary Syndrome Risk**

**Authors:** Abdul Jalil Tamjid, Tressha Rani Robi Das, Abdur Rakib, Mohammad Motiur Rahman, **Md. Mustakim Mia**, and Lutfor Rahaman

Polycystic Ovary Syndrome (PCOS) is a common endocrine disorder affecting women of reproductive age, where early diagnosis remains challenging due to diverse clinical manifestations and complex physiological interactions. This study proposes an interpretable hybrid framework that combines stacking ensemble learning with Fuzzy Cognitive Maps (FCMs) for accurate and explainable PCOS risk prediction.

The predictive model integrates Logistic Regression, Random Forest, and Support Vector Machine as base learners, with a Random Forest meta-learner, enabling effective modeling of nonlinear relationships among clinical, hormonal, and lifestyle features. Experimental results demonstrate excellent performance, achieving **98.77% accuracy**, **1.00 precision**, **0.9818 recall**, **0.9908 F1-score**, and **0.9909 AUC**.

To improve transparency, feature importance analysis and SHAP explanations were employed to identify the most influential predictors, including follicle count, hair growth, skin darkening, menstrual irregularity, and weight gain. These insights were further incorporated into an eleven-node Fuzzy Cognitive Map to model causal relationships among risk factors and support sensitivity and convergence analysis. Additionally, the proposed framework was deployed as a web-based clinical decision support tool for real-time PCOS risk assessment, providing healthcare practitioners with both accurate predictions and interpretable reasoning.

**Keywords:** Polycystic Ovary Syndrome (PCOS), Stacking Ensemble Learning, Fuzzy Cognitive Maps, Explainable AI, Clinical Decision Support Systems.
